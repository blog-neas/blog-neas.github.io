About me

The following bio may be used in media releases without further permission.

Lucio Palazzo obtained a PhD in Statistical Methods from the doctoral school Economics and Policy Analysis of Markets and Firms of the University of Salerno in May 2019. Before that, he completed an MSc in Statistics (2018) at the KU Leuven, in Belgium. Moreover, he also earned an MSc in Statistical Science for Decision Making (2015) and a BSc in Mathematics (2012), both at the University of Naples Federico II. Currently he is a Research Fellow and Lecturer of the Department of Political Sciences at University of Naples Federico II in Napoli. Lucio actively teaches, advises and supervises graduates.

Main Disciplines
introduction to statistics, statistical inference, statistical models and methods, statistical learning, computational statistics, econometrics, data visualization and storytelling, statistical softwares (R and Python).
Main Research Topics
statistical data analysis, count data, relational data, directional data, spatial data, GLM, mixture models, non-linear models, network analysis, time series analysis, classification, clustering, R and Python programming.
